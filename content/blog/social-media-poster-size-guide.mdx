## Why Poster Sizes Matter

**Social media poster dimensions** matter because:

- **Cropping** — Wrong aspect ratio can cut off important text or visuals  
- **Quality** — Too small and images look blurry; too large can slow loading  
- **Consistency** — Same sizes across posts make your feed look polished  

This **poster size guide** uses the most common and up-to-date recommendations.

## Instagram Sizes

- **Feed square:** 1080×1080 px  
- **Feed portrait:** 1080×1350 px  
- **Feed landscape:** 1080×566 px  
- **Story / Reels:** 1080×1920 px  

Use the **social media poster size** that matches your post type so nothing gets cropped awkwardly.

## Facebook, TikTok, LinkedIn

- **Facebook feed:** 1200×630 px (link preview) or 1080×1080  
- **Facebook Story:** 1080×1920 px  
- **TikTok:** 1080×1920 px (vertical)  
- **LinkedIn feed:** 1200×627 px or 1080×1080  
- **LinkedIn background:** 1584×396 px  

When in doubt, **1080×1080** works for many feed posts across platforms.

## FAQ

### What size should a social media poster be?

For feed posts, 1080×1080 (square) or 1080×1350 (portrait) work well on Instagram and Facebook. Stories and Reels use 1080×1920.

### What is the best resolution for social media posters?

Use at least 1080 px on the shortest side. Many tools export at 1080 or 1200 px; 2x (e.g. 2160) can look sharper on high-DPI devices.

### Do all platforms use the same poster size?

No. Instagram Story and TikTok use 1080×1920; LinkedIn feed often uses 1200×627 or 1080×1080. Check this guide or each platform’s help section for the latest.

### Can one poster work for multiple platforms?

Yes, if you use a safe size like 1080×1080. For Stories/Reels you need 1080×1920. Some tools let you export the same design in multiple sizes.
