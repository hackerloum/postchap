**African small businesses** often run on tight budgets. **Free marketing tools** let you:

- Create **professional posters** and graphics without a designer  
- Post consistently on social media  
- See what’s working (basic analytics)  
- Try tools before paying  

**Free marketing tools for African business** are especially valuable when they work on mobile and support local use cases.

### 1. ArtMaster — AI Poster Generator

**ArtMaster** generates on-brand posters from your brand kit. Free tier includes a set number of posters per month. Built for **African businesses** with local occasions. [Try free](https://artmasterpro.com/signup).

### 2. Canva — Templates and Design

**Canva** offers free templates for social posts, flyers, and more. Edit text and images; export for web or print. Great for one-off designs.

### 3. WhatsApp Business — Messaging and Status

**WhatsApp Business** is free and lets you set a profile, use Status for updates, and manage chats. Essential for **African small business** customer contact.

### 4. Facebook Business Suite — Facebook and Instagram

**Facebook Business Suite** (free) lets you post to Facebook and Instagram, see basic insights, and manage messages in one place.

### 5. Google Business Profile — Local Search

**Google Business Profile** is free and helps you appear in local search and on Google Maps. Add hours, photos, and updates.

### 6. Instagram (and Insights) — Visual Presence

**Instagram** plus built-in **Insights** (free) show reach and engagement. Use it with a poster maker to keep your feed consistent.

### 7. Buffer or Later (free tiers) — Scheduling

**Buffer** and **Later** offer free plans to schedule posts. Batch your content and free up time.

### 8. Google Analytics — Website Traffic

If you have a website, **Google Analytics** (free) shows how visitors find you and what they view. Use it to improve your site and offers.

### 9. Mailchimp (free tier) — Email

**Mailchimp** free tier lets you build a list and send emails. Useful for newsletters and promos when you have a website or signup form.

### 10. Trello or Notion (free) — Content Planning

**Trello** or **Notion** (free) help you plan content, ideas, and campaigns. Simple boards or pages keep **marketing** organized.
