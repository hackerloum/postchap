## Why Schools Need Social Media

**Schools and tutoring centers** use social media to reach parents on Facebook, WhatsApp, and Instagram; share success stories and events; announce open days and enrollment; and build trust with a professional presence. **School social media marketing** in Africa works best when you post regularly and speak to parents' concerns (results, safety, value).

## Set Up Your School's Social Presence

Choose 1–2 platforms (often Facebook and WhatsApp or Instagram). Use a clear profile: name, logo, location, and "Enroll" or "Contact" link. Use the same **brand** colors and style in every poster and post so parents recognize you.

## What to Post

**School social media** content that works: student success (testimonials, results, with permission); open day and enrollment (dates, how to register); behind-the-scenes (classroom, events, staff); tips for parents (study tips, exam prep); holidays and occasions (back to school, end of term); fees and programs (clear posters for each program or term). ## FAQ

### Which social media is best for schools in Africa?

Facebook and WhatsApp are widely used by parents. Instagram can work for younger, urban parents. Start with one and expand.

### What should a school post on social media?

Success stories, open days, enrollment info, tips for parents, and event photos. Keep it professional and focused on results and trust.

### How do schools get more enrollment from social media?

Post regularly, share testimonials and results, announce open days and enrollment clearly, and make it easy to contact or register (link, WhatsApp, form).
